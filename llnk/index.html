UPDATES ISSUED BY MINISTRY OF CORPORATE AFFAIRS (“MCA”)

Clarification on CSR contribution to PM CARES Fund
The MCA has vide notification dated May 26, 2020 included contribution to the ‘Prime  Minister’s... More>>
The MCA has vide notification dated May 26, 2020 included contribution to the ‘Prime  Minister’s  Citizen  Assistance and  Relief  in  Emergency  Situations  Fund (“PM CARES Fund”)’ in item (viii) of the Schedule VII to the Companies Act, 2013 (“Act”), which means that any contribution to the PM CARES Fund would qualify as corporate social responsibility expenditure under the Act. This notification has been brought into effect retrospectively from March 28,2020.

Scheme for relaxation of time for filing forms related to creation or modification of charges under

The MCA has vide circular dated June 17, 2020 introduced a scheme for relaxation of time More>>

The MCA has vide circular dated June 17, 2020 introduced a scheme for relaxation of time for filing forms relating to creation or modification of charges ( i.e. forms CHG-1 and form CHG-9 ) under the Act.

The said scheme is applicable to filing of Form No. CHG – 1 and Form No. CHG-9 by a company or a charge holder where the date of creation/modification of the charge:

  1. is before March 1, 2020, but the timeline for filing such form had not expired under section 77 of the Act (Duty to Register Charges, etc.) as on March 1, 2020; or
    2.  
  2. falls on any date between March 1, 2020 to September 30, 2020 (both dates inclusive).
Below are some features of the scheme:
Original time period for creation/ modification of the charge Before March 1, 2020 Between March 1, 2020 to September 30, 2020
Relaxation of time In case the form is not filed before March 1, 2020, the period beginning from March 1, 2020 and ending on September 30, 2020 is not to be reckoned for the purpose of counting the number of days under Section 77 or Section 78 (Application for Registration of Charge) of the Act (i.e. 120 days).

In such a case the first day after February 29, 2020 shall be reckoned as October 1, 2020 for the purpose of counting days within which the form is required to be filed under section 77 or Section 78 of the Act. 		In case the form is not filed before September 30, 2020, the period beginning from the date of creation/modification of charge to September 30, 2020 shall not to be reckoned for the purpose of counting the number of days under Section 77 or Section 78 of the Act (i.e. 120 days).

In such a case, the first day after the date of creation/modification of charge for the filing will be reckoned as October 1, 2020.
Applicable fees If the form is filed on or before September 30, 2020, the fees payable as on February 29, 2020 under the applicable rules for the said form shall be payable.

If the form is filed thereafter, the applicable fees shall be charged under the applicable rules after adding the number of days beginning from October 01, 2020 and ending on the date of filing plus the time period lapsed from the date of the creation of charge till February 29, 2020. 		If the form is filed before September 30, 2020, normal fees shall be payable under the applicable rules.

If the form is filed thereafter, the first day after the date of creation/ modification of charge shall be reckoned as October 01, 2020 and the number of days till the date of filing of the form shall be counted accordingly for the purposes of payment of fees under the applicable rules.
The scheme is not applicable in the following cases:
  1. where forms CHG-1 and CHG-9 have already been filed before the date of this circular, i.e. June 17, 2020;
  2. the timeline for filing the requisite form under Section 77 or Section 78 of the Companies Act expired prior to March 1, 2020;
  3. the timeline for filing the form expires at a future date, despite exclusion of the time provided under the header ‘Relaxation of time’ in the table above; and
  4. filing of Form CHG-4 for satisfaction of charges.

Companies (Share Capital and Debentures) Amendment Rules 2020
MCA has, vide notification dated June 5, 2020 notified the Companies (Share Capital and Debentures) More>>
MCA has, vide notification dated June 5, 2020 notified the Companies (Share Capital and Debentures) Amendment Rules, 2020 which has made the following amendments to the Companies (Share Capital and Debentures) Rules, 2014 (“Share Capital Rules”):
i. Startups
A startup company, as defined in notification number G.S.R. 127(E), dated the 19th February, 2019 issued by the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade, Ministry of Commerce and Industry, Government of lndia, may issue sweat equity shares not exceeding 50% (fifty percent) of its paid up capital up to 10 (ten) years from the date of its incorporation or registration. Prior to the amendment sweat equity shares were permitted to be issued up to 5 (five) years from the date of incorporation or registration of a start-up.
ii. Debentures
A company for the purpose of issuing debentures is required to invest or deposit 15% (fifteen per cent) of the value of its debentures maturing during the year ending on the 31st day of March of the next year in accordance with the conditions prescribed under the Share Capital Rules.
MCA vide its amendment has made it applicable for public issue of debentures by (a) listed NBFCs registered with Reserve Bank of India under section 45- IA of the RBI Act, 1934; (b) listed Housing Finance Companies registered with National Housing Bank; and (c) listed companies other than those who are already exempted from maintaining any debenture redemption reserve, to invest or deposit on or before the 30th day of April in each year, as the case may be, a sum which shall not be less than 15%, of the amount of its debentures maturing during the year, ending on the 31st day of March of the next year in any one or more methods of investments or deposits as provided in sub-clause (vi):
Provided that the amount remaining invested or deposited, as the case may be, shall not any time fall below fifteen percent. of the amount of the debentures maturing during the year ending on 31st day of March of that year.

The MCA, vide notification dated March 19, 2020, had notified the Companies (Meetings of Board and its Powers) Amendment Rules, 2020 according to which companies were permitted to conduct board meetings till June 30, 2020 to discuss matters provided under section 173 (2) of the Act read with rule 4 of the Companies (Meetings of Board and its Powers) Rules, 2014 including approval of the annual financial statements, Board’s report, approval of prospectus, the audit committee meetings for consideration of accounts, approval of matters relating to amalgamation, merger, demerger, acquisition and takeover, through video conferencing or other audio visual means. Contributions towards the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) and Central Paramilitary Forces (CPMF) veterans and their dependents including widows to fall within the definition of Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)
The MCA has vide notification dated June 23, 2020 further amended More>>
The MCA has vide notification dated June 23, 2020 further amended Schedule VII of the Act, pursuant to which contributions towards the Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF) and Central Para Military Forces (CPMF) veterans and their dependents including widows, would qualify as corporate social responsibility expenditure under the Act.
The notification has widened the scope of CSR, which was earlier restricted to activities benefiting the army, navy and air force veterans and their dependents and war widows.

The MCA has amended the Companies (Removal of Names of Companies from the Registrar of Companies) Rules, 2016, pursuant to which the MCA has further extended the period for names reserved and resubmission of forms, as follows:

UPDATES ISSUED BY SECURITIES AND EXCHANGE BOARD OF INDIA (“SEBI”)

Further extension of relaxation for time gap between two board/ audit committee meetings
In light of the COVID-19 pandemic, SEBI has, vide its circular dated June 26, 2020 More>>
In light of the COVID-19 pandemic, SEBI has, vide its circular dated June 26, 2020, extended the maximum stipulated time gap of 120 (one hundred and twenty) days between two meetings of the board of directors and audit committees of listed entities till July 31, 2020. However, the board of directors and audit committees of listed entities are required to ensure that they meet at least four times a year, as stipulated under Regulations 17(2) and 18(2)(a) of the SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015 (“LODR Regulations”).

Further extension of time for submission of Annual Secretarial Compliance (“ASC”) Report
SEBI had previously extended the timeline for submission of the annual secretarial compliance report More>>
SEBI had previously extended the timeline for submission of the annual secretarial compliance report by listed companies for the year 2019-2020 by 1 (one) month i.e. from May 31, 2020 to June 30, 2020. SEBI has now, vide a circular SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD1/CIR/P/2020/109 dated June 25, 2020, further extended such timeline from June 30, 2020 to July 31, 2020.

Further extension of time for submission of financial results for the quarter/ half year/ financial year ending 31st March 2020
Due to the ongoing and continuing impact of COVID-19 pandemic More>>
Due to the ongoing and continuing impact of COVID-19 pandemic, SEBI has, vide circular SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD1/CIR/P/2020/106 dated June 24, 2020, further extended the timeline for submission of financial results for the quarter and year ending March 31, 2020 by listed companies under Regulation 33 of the LODR Regulations, by an additional one month, to July 31, 2020. Similarly, the timeline under Regulation 52 of the LODR Regulations for submission of half-yearly and/or annual financial results for the period ending March 31, 2020, for entities that have listed NCDs, NCRPS’, CPs, MDS’ is also extended to July 31, 2020.

Relaxations for Listed Companies having stressed assets
SEBI has vide a press release no. 35/ 2020 dated June 23, 2020, decided to relax the pricing methodology More>>
SEBI has vide a press release no. 35/ 2020 dated June 23, 2020, decided to relax the pricing methodology for preferential issue of shares by listed companies having stressed assets and exempt allottees of preferential issues from open offer obligations with immediate effect. Eligible listed companies having stressed assets will now be able to determine the pricing of their preferential allotments at not less than the average of weekly high and low of the volume-weighted average prices of related equity shares during the two weeks preceding the relevant date. Further, SEBI has exempted allottees in such issuances from making an open offer even if the threshold of acquisition triggers an open offer or there is a change in control in terms of SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011 (“Takeover Regulations”).

Relaxations under SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018 (“ICDR Regulations”)
SEBI has, vide a circular dated June 9, 2020, decided to provide relaxations for further public offers (FPOs) More>>
SEBI has, vide a circular dated June 9, 2020, decided to provide relaxations for further public offers (FPOs). These include relaxations from certain provisions of the ICDR Regulations in respect of eligibility conditions for FPOs, including reducing the minimum average market capitalization of public shareholding requirement from INR 1000 crore to INR 500 crore. However, these relaxations are temporary in nature and are applicable only to FPOs that are opened on or before March 31, 2021 and would not be applicable for issuance of warrants.

Revision to post-default curing period for Credit Rating Agencies (“CRA”)
SEBI has, vide circular dated May 21, 2020, revised the post-default curing period specified More>>
SEBI has, vide circular dated May 21, 2020, revised the post-default curing period specified under the Enhanced Standards for credit rating agencies (CRAs) in its circular dated November 1, 2016. The circular, inter-alia, states that after a default is cured and the payments regularized, the CRA should generally upgrade the rating of a company from default to non-investment grade after a period of 90 days based on the satisfactory performance by the company during the said period. However, CRAs may deviate from the period of 90 days on case to case basis subject to framing a detailed policy in this regard and the placing the said policy on the website of the CRA.

Relaxations for takeovers and buy-back
In view of the continuing COVID-19 pandemic situation, SEBI has, vide a circular dated May 14, 2020 More>>
In view of the continuing COVID-19 pandemic situation, SEBI has, vide a circular dated May 14, 2020, granted certain one-time relaxations from enforcement of certain regulations of Takeover Regulations and SEBI (Buy-back of Securities) Regulations, 2018 pertaining to dispatch of letter of offer and/or tender form and other related material for open offers and buy-back tender offers opening up to July 31, 2020. Pursuant to the aforesaid circular, companies have been permitted to send the letter of offer through electronic mode, subject to compliance with certain prescribed conditions.

Relaxation from the Minimum Public Shareholding (MPS) requirements
SEBI has, vide circular dated May 14, 2020, decided to grant relaxation from MPS requirements More>>
SEBI has, vide circular dated May 14, 2020, decided to grant relaxation from MPS requirements stipulated in its previous circular dated October 10, 2017 for listed entities for whom the deadline to comply with MPS requirements falls between the period from March 1, 2020 to August 31, 2020. The October 10, 2017 circular detailed the actions that could be taken by the recognized stock exchanges/depositories against listed entities who are in non-compliance with the MPS requirements under LODR Regulations. Further, the circular requires all penal actions, if any, initiated by Stock Exchanges from March 1, 2020 till date for non- compliance of MPS requirements by such listed entities to be withdrawn.

Additional relaxations under LODR Regulations
SEBI, vide circular dated May 12, 2020, granted relaxation to listed entities More>>
SEBI, vide circular dated May 12, 2020, granted relaxation to listed entities from certain procedural requirements under LODR Regulations. Relaxations have been provided from: (a) sending physical copies of annual report to shareholders till December 31, 2020; (b) sending proxy forms for general meetings if the meeting is held electronically till December 31, 2020; (c) requirement of sending ‘payable-at-par’ warrants/ cheques for dividends in excess of Rs. 1500/- by speed post; (d) publication of advertisements in the newspapers for events scheduled till June 30, 2020; and (e) publishing quarterly consolidated financial results for listed banking and insurance companies for the quarter ending June 30, 2020.

Relaxations relating to procedural matters pertaining to issues and listing

SEBI has, vide a circular dated May 6, 2020, granted one-time relaxation More>>
SEBI has, vide a circular dated May 6, 2020, granted one-time relaxation to listed entities from strict enforcement of certain procedural requirements pertaining to rights issue taking place up to July 31, 2020 under ICDR Regulations. The relaxations provided, inter alia, provide that listed entities may (a) undertake service of the abridged letter of offer for rights issue, application form and other issue material to shareholders by way of electronic transmission; (b) make use of website of the issuer, registrar, lead managers, and stock exchanges, television channels, radio, internet etc. to disseminate information relating to the application process; (c) in case the physical shareholders who have not been able to open a demat account or are unable to communicate their demat details to the issuer/ registrar for credit of rights entitlement within specified time, such physical shareholders are to be allowed to submit their application subject to compliance with prescribed conditions; (d) apart from ASBA facility, the issuer shall provide an optional non-cash mechanism to accept applications from shareholders subject to ensuring that no third party payments shall be allowed in respect of any application.

SEBI notifies entities permitted to undertake e-KYC Aadhaar authentication service of Unique Identification Authority of India (“UIDAI”)

Bombay Stock Exchange Limited, National Securities Depository Limited, Central Depository Services (India) Limited, CDSL Ventures Limited, NSDL Database Management Limited, NSE Data and Analytics Limited, CAMS Investor Services Private Limited and Computer Age Management Services Private Limited are SEBI’s notified reporting entities (collectively “KUA” i.e. KYC user agency), which are permitted to undertake e-KYC Aadhaar authentication service of UIDAI.

Framework for Regulatory Sandbox

SEBI has, vide circular dated June 5, 2020, decided to introduce a framework for Regulatory Sandbox, under which, entities regulated by SEBI shall be granted certain facilities and flexibilities to experiment with financial technology solutions in a live environment and on limited set of real customers for a limited time frame.

Amendment to Takeover Regulation to enhance the creeping acquisition limit for promoters of a listed company
SEBI has vide notification dated June 16, 2020 amended the Takeover Regulations to enhance More>>
SEBI has vide notification dated June 16, 2020 amended the Takeover Regulations to enhance the creeping acquisition limit for promoters of a listed company. The amendment allows a shareholder owning 25% or more of the shares or voting rights in a company to increase his shareholding by up to 10% in a year against the earlier limit of 5%. But this increase in limit is permitted only via a preferential issue of equity shares.
The pertinent provision in the Takeovers Regulations provide for shareholders to increase their shares or voting rights in a company, by up to a specified threshold, in order to consolidate their shareholding. Any increase in voting rights beyond the permitted threshold will result in a mandatory open offer, according to regulation 3(2) of the Takeover Regulations. The threshold has been raised from 5% to 10%, only for the financial year 2020-21. SEBI has also relaxed the provision for voluntary open offer. Earlier, a shareholder holding 25% or more of shares or voting rights was permitted to make a open offer, but only if such shareholder had not acquired any shares of the company via the creeping acquisition route in the preceding 52 weeks. This condition has now been relaxed till March 31, 2021.

Voluntary Retention Route (“VRR”) for Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs)
RBI, vide RBI/2019-20/239 A.P.(DIR Series) Circular No.32 dated May 22, 2020, allowed FPIs More>>
RBI, vide RBI/2019-20/239 A.P.(DIR Series) Circular No.32 dated May 22, 2020, allowed FPIs that have been allotted investment limits, between January 24, 2020 (the date of reopening of allotment of investment limits) and April 30, 2020, an additional time of 3 (three) months to invest 75% of their Committed Portfolio Size (“CPS”, a requirement as per RBI notification dated May 24, 2019).
